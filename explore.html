Things to do with your dog abroad

There is more to a dog-friendly holiday than just relaxing on the beach

Dog Friendly Activities in Hungary

Balatonfoldvar

Why not explore the Visitor Center of Shipping of Balaton, where dogs are welcome, and learn about the history of ships? The museum features an interactive exhibition, cinema screenings, a café and a viewing tower with amazing views of lake Balaton.

Fonyod

For a perfect day out with your dog, you could take a comp from to Fonyod to Badacsony – dogs are welcome on board – and enjoy the gastronomy of this wonderful region. You could visit Laposa Winery that offers wine tasting sessions with lunch or dinner and allows dogs to stay on the premises.

Keszthely

You could visit the Georgikon Agricultural Museum, which houses an agricultural exhibition and a modern farm. Guided tours and existing programs for children are also available, and dogs are allowed on the premises if kept on leash.

Dog Friendly Activities in Barcelona

Water park Perros al Agua

Take your dog for a swim in one of the largest pet friendly swimming pools in Spain. There is also shallow water for small dogs and a restaurant for humans.

Parc Guell

You can bring your dog into one of the best known urban parks in Barcelona designed by Gaudi and enjoy lots of paths and garderns with your best friend.

Mercat de Sant Josep de la Boqueria

The most popular food market in Barcelona is pet friendly! Come to la Boqueria with your best friend and explore the market with the freshest food in Barcelona.

Dog Friendly Activities in Paris

Jardin du Luxembourg

Jardin du Luxembourg is one of the most visited parks in Paris. So, we would have been disappointed if we couldn’t visit this place when traveling with dogs. But no worries, it is pet friendly in the east side of the park.

Champs de Mars

When traveling with dogs, Eiffel Tower is one of the must visit. We are luckly that Champs de Mars allow dogs because now they can enjoy breath-taking view of Eiffer Tower while having their favourite snacks. Your pooch can also interact with their friends around the area.

Double-decker sightseeing bus

Yes! Dogs are allowed in those double-decker sightseeing buses you often see in Paris and they are pet friendly! This is a must when traveling with dogs for both you and your pooch because the buses will take you to every famous tourist attraction in Paris.
