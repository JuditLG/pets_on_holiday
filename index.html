Hungary - Lake Balaton

Lake Balaton and its region is a major holiday destination with beaches, volcanic hills, wineries and lively coastal towns.

There is plenty of accommodation to choose from, depending on whether you are looking to stay in a hotel with wellness facilities or staying in a private, self-contained apartment.

Our recommendations

Balatonfoldvar opened a dog-friendly beach this summer, allowing you to enjoy the cooling down in the water with your dog on a hot summer day. Szandra Haz, a self-contained apartment house with garden and swimming pool is a short drive from the dog-friendly beach.

Fonyod

Fonyod also offers a dog-friendly beach, where you can swim with your dog and enjoy the sunshine. Tappancs (Paws) apartment house is within a short walking distance, and needless to say, dogs are welcome.

While there is no dog-friendly beach in Keszthely, Abbazia Club Hotel offers dog-friendly accommodation with wellness facilities. Spain - Costa Maresme

One of the most beautiful coastal areas in Europe with spectacular award-winning beaches and a vibrant culture.

Our recommendations

Bell Soll Campsite

Right in the seafront of Pineda de Mar, 49 km from Barcelona, Bell Soll Campsite is set just few minutes’ walk away from a sandy pet-friendly beach open all year around and without time restrictions. The site has an outdoor pool, a playground for kids and communal barbecues. Just off site in Pineda de Mar you can find a wide variety of restaurants, tapas bars and natural parks to visit.

Book now

Hotel Subur Maritim

Hotel Subur Maritim is a 4-star pet-friendly hotel situated in the charming town of Sitges. The accommodation features a large sea-view terrace, air conditioning in all rooms and an outdoor swimming pool. The hotel is 5.9 Km from Cala Vallcarca, one of the most popular pet-friendly beaches in the province of Barcelona. It is surrounded by natural parks and is open all year around and without time restrictions.

Book now

Lyon Hotel

Lyon hotel is a bright and cozy option located in Barcelona just a 3-minutes walk from the tube station Barceloneta. Ideal if you want to move around fast with your dog in Barcelona. Next to the hotel there is a park called Parc de la Barceloneta which has a doggy play area.

Book now

Tips

Plan in advance

Especially during summer season, accomodations can get fully booked fairly quickly. The Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs and Animal and Plant Health Agency recommends to have all procedures done by at least 4 months in advance. Don’t forget to check rules and local regulations that apply for dogs on public places at your holiday destination.

Pet passport

Check with your vet that your pet is up-to date on all the necessary vaccines and procedures based on your destination. Your vet should be able to issue a pet passport. If not, you can contact the Animal & Plant health Agency for more information on how obtain one.

Pet’s health insurance

Don’t forget to check with your pet insurance whether your pet will be covered at your holiday destination. Some pet insurances offer the option to purchase additional cover for overseas trips.

Transporting your pet

Decide whether your pet will be travelling by air, ferry, train or car and carefully consider what will be involved with your pet during the journey. Make sure your pet will be comfortable, safe and with water and food available if need be. Always do a trial weekend first to ensure the environment will work out well for your dog.

Assistance dogs

Different rules apply for travelling with assistance dogs. Always check with your airline in advance if you are planning to travel with your dog by air. For example, assistance dogs can travel in the cabin free of charge with British Airways.
